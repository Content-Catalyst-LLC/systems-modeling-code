# Responsible Use

Organizational systems models can influence staffing, performance evaluation, surveillance, automation, restructuring, promotion, discipline, compensation, workload allocation, and organizational redesign. They should improve learning, fairness, accountability, and humane organizational design.

Do not use these synthetic workflows for real decisions without:

- Privacy review
- Data minimization
- Consent and governance
- Stakeholder participation
- Equity assessment
- Qualitative validation
- Clear communication of limitations
- Human review
- Protection against punitive misuse

Organizational systems modeling should support understanding. It should not automate control over people.
