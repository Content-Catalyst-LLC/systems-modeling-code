# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- The core model uses the stylized equation dx/dt = r + x - x^3.
- The control parameter changes gradually along forward and backward paths.
- Approximate transitions are detected from large step-to-step state changes.
- Rolling variance and autocorrelation are illustrative early-warning diagnostics.
- Hysteresis is assessed by comparing forward and backward paths.

## Limitations

- No empirical ecological, climate, infrastructure, financial, health, organizational, public policy, or social system is calibrated.
- The equation is a teaching model, not a universal tipping model.
- Real systems may tip through bifurcation, noise-induced, rate-induced, network, or behavioral mechanisms.
- Early-warning signals are not guaranteed.
- Applied use requires domain data, validation, sensitivity analysis, stakeholder review, and careful communication.
