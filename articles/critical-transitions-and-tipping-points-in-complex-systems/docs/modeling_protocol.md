# Critical Transition and Tipping-Point Modeling Protocol

Critical-transition modeling treats nonlinear stability, thresholds, regime shifts, hysteresis, feedback amplification, and early-warning indicators as explicit analytical objects.

## Core questions

- What is the focal system and desired regime?
- Which control parameters change slowly?
- What feedback structures stabilize or destabilize the system?
- Are there alternative stable states?
- Does the system contain collapse and recovery thresholds?
- Is hysteresis expected?
- Which early-warning signals can be monitored?
- Are there network dependencies or cascade pathways?
- How uncertain are thresholds and mechanisms?
- What intervention timing reduces the probability of harmful transition?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline hysteresis, slow forcing, fast forcing, wide forcing, near-threshold starting conditions, recovery stress, high-resolution forcing, and policy-delay scenarios.
