# Modeling Principles

A systems model should be explicit about:

1. Purpose — explanation, exploration, forecasting, decision support, learning, or communication.
2. Boundary — what is included, excluded, simplified, or treated as external.
3. Structure — stocks, flows, agents, events, networks, feedback, delays, constraints, and dependencies.
4. Evidence — data, expert judgment, assumptions, priors, calibration targets, and validation references.
5. Uncertainty — parameter uncertainty, structural uncertainty, scenario uncertainty, and interpretive ambiguity.
6. Validation — tests appropriate to the model's intended use.
7. Interpretation — what the model can and cannot support.
8. Ethics — who defines the model, whose knowledge counts, and who carries risk if the model is wrong.
