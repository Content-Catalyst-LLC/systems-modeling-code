# Responsible Use

Integrated assessment models can influence climate policy, energy transition planning, carbon budgets, infrastructure investment, land-use strategy, development pathways, and intergenerational decision-making. IAM outputs should clarify pathways and tradeoffs. They should not obscure justice, uncertainty, or value choices behind equations.

Do not use these synthetic workflows for real policy decisions without formal model documentation, empirical calibration, sensitivity analysis, uncertainty analysis, model comparison, distributional review, transparent discounting, land-use review, technology feasibility review, public communication of limits, and human accountability.
