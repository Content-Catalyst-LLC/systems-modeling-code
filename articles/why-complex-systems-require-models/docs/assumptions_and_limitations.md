# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- The system state can be summarized as a scalar variable.
- Reinforcing feedback increases inflow based on current state.
- Balancing feedback responds to delayed state.
- Threshold behavior activates after the state crosses a defined level.
- A discrete shock can be introduced at a specified time.
- Scenario comparison reveals how structural assumptions shape behavior.

## Limitations

- The model is synthetic.
- It is not calibrated to empirical observations.
- The state variable is abstract.
- Real complex systems may require multiple variables, networks, agents, events, spatial structure, and institutional dynamics.
- Outputs should be interpreted as workflow demonstrations, not empirical findings.
