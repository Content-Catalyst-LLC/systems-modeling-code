# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- Agents are synthetic and grouped into three simplified segments.
- Adoption is binary and irreversible.
- Peer influence travels through synthetic network ties.
- Adoption follows a deterministic threshold rule.
- Seed adopters begin diffusion.
- Intervention effects are represented as parameter changes.

## Limitations

- No empirical calibration.
- No real social network data.
- No abandonment or switching.
- No multi-stage adoption process.
- No competing innovations.
- No geography.
- No media, regulation, pricing, or supply-chain system.
- No full equity, trust, or legitimacy model.

This is a learning model, not a validated adoption forecast.
