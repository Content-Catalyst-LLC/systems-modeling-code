# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- Exponential, logistic, and delayed-feedback structures can illustrate major historical modeling ideas.
- Scenario comparison can show how model structure shapes behavior.
- Delayed balancing feedback is a useful simplified representation of system-dynamics reasoning.
- Synthetic outputs can demonstrate workflow design without claiming empirical validity.

## Limitations

- The workflows do not reproduce original historical models.
- The model is scalar and simplified.
- No empirical calibration is included.
- Real systems may require multiple interacting stocks, agents, networks, events, sectors, and spatial structure.
- Results should be interpreted as examples of modeling logic, not historical findings.
