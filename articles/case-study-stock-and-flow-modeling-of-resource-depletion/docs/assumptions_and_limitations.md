# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- The resource is represented as one aggregate stock.
- Regeneration follows a logistic growth function.
- Demand grows at a scenario-specific rate.
- Conservation responds to scarcity.
- Extraction is constrained by demand, available stock, and extraction capacity.
- A critical threshold flags severe depletion.

## Limitations

- No spatial variation.
- No stakeholder groups or distributional effects.
- No stochastic shocks.
- No formal calibration.
- No institutional enforcement model.
- No detailed market or price system.
- No domain-specific ecology, hydrology, geology, or social context.

This is a learning model, not a validated management tool.
