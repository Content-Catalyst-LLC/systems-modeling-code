# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- The core time-series model is autoregressive with a gradually changing stability parameter.
- Higher stability values represent weakening recovery.
- Rolling variance and lag-1 autocorrelation are proxy indicators of critical slowing down.
- Scenario differences represent noise, monitoring-window, and destabilization-rate assumptions.
- Spatial and network examples are taxonomies, not calibrated empirical analyses.

## Limitations

- No empirical ecological, climate, infrastructure, financial, health, organizational, public policy, or social system is calibrated.
- Early-warning signals can produce false positives and false negatives.
- Real systems may collapse through mechanisms not represented by critical slowing down.
- Rolling diagnostics depend on window size, preprocessing, sampling frequency, and noise.
- Applied use requires domain evidence, validation, model comparison, sensitivity analysis, and governance review.
