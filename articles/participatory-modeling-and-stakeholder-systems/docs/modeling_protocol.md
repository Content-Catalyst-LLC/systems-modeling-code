# Participatory Modeling Protocol

Participatory modeling connects formal systems modeling with stakeholder knowledge, local knowledge, operational knowledge, institutional judgment, lived experience, and shared interpretation.

## Core questions

- What decision, learning process, conflict, or planning challenge is the model meant to support?
- Who defines the problem?
- Who is affected by the model and its use?
- Who has knowledge that is not represented in formal datasets?
- Who has authority to act on model outputs?
- What is open to stakeholder influence and what is already fixed?
- What assumptions are documented, contested, or excluded?
- How are disagreement, missing voices, and power differences recorded?
- How will the model be validated technically and socially?
- Who owns the model after the process ends?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare stakeholder-weighted scenario scores, disagreement diagnostics, assumption registers, and legitimacy-adjusted scenario rankings.
