# Systems Modeling: Notes

Systems modeling translates conceptual understanding of complex systems into formal and computational representations.

## Core Modeling Questions

- What is the purpose of the model?
- What are the system boundaries?
- What are the stocks, flows, agents, nodes, or events?
- What feedback loops shape system behavior?
- Are delays, nonlinearities, thresholds, or shocks represented?
- What assumptions determine the model's behavior?
- How sensitive are outputs to uncertain parameters?
- What evidence supports the relationships?
- What should not be inferred from the model?

## Interpretation Cautions

- A simulation is not a prediction by default.
- A model boundary can hide important causes.
- Precise outputs can create false confidence.
- Feedback loops can be misspecified.
- Missing delays can distort behavior.
- Stakeholder context matters.
