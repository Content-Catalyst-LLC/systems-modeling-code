# Environmental Systems Modeling Protocol

Environmental systems modeling treats stocks, flows, stressors, receptors, pathways, feedback loops, spatial scale, temporal scale, scenarios, interventions, and distributional risk as explicit analytical objects.

## Core questions

- What environmental process is the model designed to explain?
- Which spatial and temporal boundaries define the model?
- Which stocks accumulate over time?
- Which flows increase, decrease, move, transform, or degrade those stocks?
- Which stressors and receptors matter?
- Through which pathways does exposure occur?
- Which feedback loops amplify or stabilize environmental change?
- Which thresholds or regime shifts are plausible?
- Which interventions change long-term trajectories?
- Who benefits, who is exposed, and who participates in interpretation?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline pressure, high extraction, restoration investment, larger disturbance, lower growth, strong intervention, high exposure weight, and low flow persistence scenarios.
