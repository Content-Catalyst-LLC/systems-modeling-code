# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- The environmental stock workflow uses logistic regeneration, extraction, restoration, and disturbance.
- The pollution workflow uses simplified load, decay, flow removal, and exposure weighting.
- Environmental justice is represented conceptually rather than through empirical demographic data.
- Scenarios are illustrative and not calibrated.

## Limitations

- No real watershed, ecosystem, pollutant source, community, climate risk, or regulatory case is represented.
- Spatial heterogeneity is conceptual rather than geospatial.
- Real pollutant fate may require chemistry, meteorology, hydrology, soil, sediment, and food-web dynamics.
- Real ecosystem recovery may involve many species, thresholds, disturbances, and governance constraints.
- Applied use requires monitoring data, calibration, validation, uncertainty analysis, community participation, and ethical review.
