# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- Agents follow simple local rules.
- Heterogeneity is represented by type, location, or threshold.
- Local neighborhoods are sufficient to demonstrate emergence.
- Scenarios can reveal sensitivity to behavioral assumptions.
- Synthetic data are adequate for workflow demonstration.

## Limitations

- Agents do not represent real people.
- Rules are simplified and not empirically calibrated.
- Spatial neighborhoods are stylized.
- Threshold adoption uses a simple ring structure.
- Results should not be interpreted as forecasts or real-world evidence.
