# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- Service availability is represented on a 0 to 1 scale.
- Power disruption propagates into communications, water, and transportation through dependency parameters.
- Load-capacity diagnostics use stylized demand and capacity trajectories.
- Recovery is simplified as a fixed rate.
- Equity dimensions are conceptual rather than empirical.

## Limitations

- No real infrastructure network, city, utility, transportation system, emergency service, or public agency is represented.
- Spatial heterogeneity is conceptual rather than geospatial.
- Actual infrastructure dependencies may be hidden, proprietary, classified, or operationally complex.
- Applied use requires engineering data, dependency audit, calibration, validation, stress testing, and community review.
