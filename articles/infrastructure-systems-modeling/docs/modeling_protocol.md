# Infrastructure Systems Modeling Protocol

Infrastructure systems modeling treats assets, networks, load, capacity, condition, interdependence, disruption, recovery, governance, and equity as explicit analytical objects.

## Core questions

- What infrastructure service is the model designed to explain?
- Which networks, assets, users, and service areas are inside the boundary?
- Which load and capacity assumptions drive service pressure?
- Which systems depend on one another?
- Which disruption scenarios are plausible?
- How does failure propagate across components or sectors?
- Which assets, users, or facilities are critical?
- How does recovery unfold over time?
- Who experiences service loss, delay, or affordability burden?
- Which investments alter resilience and long-term trajectory?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline cascade, larger power loss, faster recovery, high digital dependency, longer shock, stronger interdependence, lower demand growth, and resilient backup scenarios.
