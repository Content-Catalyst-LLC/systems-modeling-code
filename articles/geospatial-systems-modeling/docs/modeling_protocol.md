# Geospatial Systems Modeling Protocol

Geospatial systems modeling connects spatial data, systems thinking, simulation, exposure analysis, accessibility, networks, uncertainty, and responsible interpretation.

## Core questions

- What spatial system is being modeled?
- What is the decision or analytical purpose?
- What spatial units define the model?
- What boundaries are used, and why?
- Which layers represent population, hazards, services, assets, infrastructure, land cover, terrain, or vulnerability?
- Which spatial relationships matter: distance, adjacency, containment, network connectivity, upstream-downstream relation, or flow?
- Which assumptions define hazard, exposure, vulnerability, accessibility, and priority?
- How sensitive are conclusions to scale, boundary, resolution, weighting, and missing data?
- Which communities, places, or informal systems may be underrepresented?
- How will uncertainty and limitations be communicated?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline, higher hazard, high vulnerability, low access, population growth, and resilient service scenarios.
