# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- Demand, capacity, backlog, burnout, attrition, access gap, and trust are simplified.
- Trust is represented on a 0 to 1 scale.
- Capacity is an effective-service index, not literal beds or clinicians.
- Surge events are stylized.
- Prevention is represented as a simplified demand reduction.
- Equity dimensions are conceptual rather than empirical.

## Limitations

- No real patient population, hospital, clinic, health system, disease, public health agency, payer, or community is represented.
- Clinical outcomes, individual health states, epidemiology, and social determinants are simplified.
- Applied use requires clinical and operational evidence, privacy governance, validation, calibration, equity review, uncertainty analysis, and stakeholder interpretation.
