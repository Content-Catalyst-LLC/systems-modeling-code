# Responsible Use

Health systems models can influence care access, staffing, triage, funding, insurance design, public health response, clinical decision support, surveillance, resource allocation, and patient experience. These decisions affect life, health, dignity, autonomy, privacy, trust, and equity.

Do not use these synthetic workflows for real decisions without:

- Clinical and operational evidence
- Privacy review
- Data minimization
- Consent and governance where appropriate
- Bias and subgroup performance review
- Equity assessment
- Clinician and public health review
- Patient and community participation where relevant
- Human accountability for decisions
- Clear communication of limitations

Health systems modeling should improve care, prevention, fairness, and accountability. It should not reduce patients, communities, or health workers to abstract optimization objects.
