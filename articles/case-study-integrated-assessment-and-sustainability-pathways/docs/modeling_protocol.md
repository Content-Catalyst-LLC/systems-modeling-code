# Integrated Assessment and Sustainability Pathways Modeling Protocol

This case models sustainability pathways as linked trajectories across energy demand, clean energy transition, emissions, cumulative emissions, climate stress, adaptation capacity, climate damages, transition cost, land pressure, water stress, equity, and sustainability diagnostics.

## Core steps

1. Define the sustainability pathway question.
2. Set the system boundary.
3. Define pathway alternatives.
4. Specify demand, clean energy, efficiency, adaptation, equity, and ecological assumptions.
5. Link energy, emissions, climate stress, adaptation, damages, land, water, and equity.
6. Simulate each pathway over time.
7. Compute sustainability diagnostics.
8. Test sensitivity and robustness.
9. Build decision-support outputs.
10. Communicate assumptions, uncertainty, values, and limits.

## Core logic

Pathways are conditional futures. They are not forecasts. Their value is in comparing linked consequences, tradeoffs, constraints, and timing.
