# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- Energy demand grows unless efficiency improves.
- Clean energy share reduces emissions intensity.
- Annual emissions accumulate into cumulative emissions.
- Cumulative emissions contribute to climate stress.
- Adaptation capacity reduces climate damages.
- Land pressure, water stress, and equity are represented as normalized diagnostics.
- Sustainability score is a transparent comparison metric, not a decision rule.

## Limitations

- No real-world calibration.
- No sector-specific emissions accounting.
- No regional climate model.
- No macroeconomic equilibrium model.
- No grid dispatch, storage, or reliability model.
- No geospatial land-use model.
- No hydrology or biodiversity model.
- No full justice, rights, or institutional legitimacy model.
- No political feasibility model.

This is a learning model, not an operational integrated assessment model.
