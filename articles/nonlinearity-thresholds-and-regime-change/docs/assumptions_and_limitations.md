# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- System state is bounded between 0 and 100.
- Pressure grows unless intervention reduces it.
- Collapse occurs when pressure crosses the collapse threshold.
- Recovery occurs only when pressure falls below a separate recovery threshold.
- Degraded regimes have stronger nonlinear damage and weaker recovery.
- Early-warning diagnostics are illustrative.

## Limitations

- No empirical ecological, climate, infrastructure, financial, health, public policy, or organizational system is calibrated.
- Thresholds are scenario assumptions, not measured boundaries.
- Early-warning signals can produce false positives or false negatives.
- No spatial or network heterogeneity is modeled in the core workflow.
- Applied use requires domain data, structural validation, threshold uncertainty analysis, and stakeholder review.
