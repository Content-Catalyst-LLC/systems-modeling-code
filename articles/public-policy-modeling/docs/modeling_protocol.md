# Public Policy Modeling Protocol

Public policy modeling treats policy levers, target populations, institutions, behavior, outcomes, side effects, feedback loops, equity, uncertainty, and implementation capacity as explicit analytical objects.

## Core questions

- What public problem is the model designed to examine?
- Which policy lever is being tested?
- Which population, institution, sector, or place is affected?
- What mechanism links policy action to outcome?
- What administrative capacity is required?
- What behavioral or strategic responses are likely?
- What side effects or unintended consequences could accumulate?
- How are benefits, burdens, risks, and voice distributed?
- Which uncertainties shape policy performance?
- How should evidence update the model and decision?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline adaptive policy, aggressive policy rule, low capacity learning, high burden design, trust-centered design, low side-effect policy, short policy window, and capacity-first policy scenarios.
