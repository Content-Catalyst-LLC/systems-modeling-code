# Economic Systems Modeling Protocol

Economic systems modeling treats agents, institutions, markets, balance sheets, stocks, flows, expectations, feedback loops, policy scenarios, and distributional effects as explicit analytical objects.

## Core questions

- What economic behavior is the model designed to explain?
- Which agents, sectors, institutions, and markets are inside the boundary?
- Which stocks accumulate over time?
- Which flows change those stocks?
- Which feedback loops amplify or stabilize activity?
- Which expectations or behavioral rules shape decisions?
- Which balance-sheet relationships constrain future behavior?
- Which shocks and policy scenarios matter?
- Which groups, sectors, or regions are affected differently?
- How sensitive are results to assumptions and uncertainty?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline feedback, higher investment, tighter credit, larger shock, higher debt growth, weak demand, rapid depreciation, and policy support scenarios.
