# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- The core workflow uses stylized demand, investment, credit, and capital dynamics.
- Households, firms, finance, government, and expectations are represented in simplified form.
- Credit growth is linked to investment.
- Fragility is represented as debt divided by capital.
- Shocks affect output through a simple demand disturbance.

## Limitations

- No real economy, country, sector, institution, market, or policy is calibrated.
- Behavioral rules are simplified.
- Distributional effects are represented conceptually rather than through empirical microdata.
- Balance sheets are stylized.
- Real economies include politics, institutions, trade, global finance, ecology, technology, and power relations not captured fully here.
- Applied use requires data, calibration, validation, uncertainty analysis, and stakeholder review.
