# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- Service level is represented as a normalized value between zero and one.
- Climate stress trajectories are synthetic.
- Exposure and sensitivity convert stress into damage pressure.
- Adaptive capacity reduces effective vulnerability.
- Recovery is represented as a simple rate.
- Degradation accumulates when stress exceeds capacity.
- Adaptation investment improves adaptive capacity.
- Transformation can be activated by a simplified trigger.

## Limitations

- No real climate hazard data.
- No downscaled climate projection.
- No geospatial exposure model.
- No social vulnerability model.
- No detailed infrastructure, ecological, or public-health mechanics.
- No operational repair logistics.
- No political economy or legal authority model.
- No empirical validation.

This is a learning model, not an operational climate resilience decision model.
