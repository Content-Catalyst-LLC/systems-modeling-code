# AI-Enhanced Systems Modeling Protocol

AI-enhanced systems modeling combines structural models with learning components. The workflow should clarify the system question, structural assumptions, data environment, AI role, validation method, governance boundaries, and communication requirements.

## Core questions

- What system behavior is being modeled?
- What does the structural model explain?
- What is the machine-learning component allowed to learn?
- Is AI estimating parameters, emulating a simulation, learning residuals, detecting anomalies, forecasting, or clustering scenarios?
- What data are used, and how were they generated?
- What bias, drift, missingness, proxy, or privacy risks are present?
- What constraints must the model respect?
- How will performance be validated across scenarios, subgroups, and stress cases?
- What human oversight and accountability are required?
- How will uncertainty and limits be communicated?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare structural baseline and hybrid residual-learning performance across baseline, high-noise, strong-residual, weak-structure, drifting-system, and low-noise scenarios.
