# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- Policy options are represented by synthetic base scores.
- Future scenarios modify those scores through multipliers.
- Metrics are normalized between zero and one.
- Metric weights are explicit and sum to one.
- Cost is treated as a negative scoring component.
- Robustness combines average score, worst-case score, and regret.

## Limitations

- No real policy data.
- No legal analysis.
- No political coalition model.
- No full equity analysis.
- No implementation timeline.
- No public participation process.
- No causal policy-effect estimation.
- No validation against real outcomes.

This is a learning model, not an operational policy recommendation engine.
