# Intervention Depth Notes

Intervention depth is not a moral ranking by itself. Deeper interventions can be powerful, but they can also be infeasible, illegitimate, destabilizing, or poorly timed.

## Diagnostic distinction

- Shallow interventions change numerical values.
- Moderate interventions change buffers, delays, or stock-flow structure.
- Structural interventions change feedback, information flow, and rule logic.
- Deep interventions change self-organization, goals, paradigms, and capacity to revise the system itself.

Professional modeling should compare behavior over time rather than assuming deeper language means deeper leverage.
