# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- The main workflow uses undirected random networks.
- Initial failures are targeted to high-degree nodes.
- Nodes become affected when affected-neighbor exposure exceeds a threshold.
- Thresholds are homogeneous within each scenario.
- Containment is represented through threshold, connectivity, and seed-size variation.

## Limitations

- No empirical infrastructure, financial, health, supply-chain, ecological, public policy, organizational, or social system is calibrated.
- Real cascades may involve directed links, weighted exposures, capacities, repair, behavior, multiplex dependencies, and geography.
- Random networks may not represent real-world network topology.
- Social contagion requires attention to agency, meaning, power, and institutions.
- Applied use requires domain data, validation, uncertainty analysis, and ethical review.
