# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- Network workflows use random undirected graphs.
- The largest connected component fraction is treated as a network order parameter.
- Link probability and average degree are treated as connectivity control parameters.
- Bifurcation examples use a stylized equation with a threshold at zero.
- Domain examples are conceptual mappings, not calibrated empirical models.

## Limitations

- No empirical physical, ecological, climate, infrastructure, financial, organizational, public policy, or social system is calibrated.
- Random-graph behavior may not represent real network topology.
- Social and institutional systems should not be treated as mechanical systems without agency, power, and meaning.
- Critical thresholds may be uncertain, moving, or context-dependent.
- Applied use requires domain data, validation, mechanism review, sensitivity analysis, and stakeholder interpretation.
