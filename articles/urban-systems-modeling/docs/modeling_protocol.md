# Urban Systems Modeling Protocol

Urban systems modeling treats population, housing, land use, transportation, infrastructure, environment, governance, equity, and spatial relationships as explicit analytical objects.

## Core questions

- What urban behavior is the model designed to explain?
- Which spatial boundary matters: block, neighborhood, corridor, city, region, or watershed?
- Which urban stocks accumulate over time?
- Which flows change those stocks?
- Which networks structure access, service, and risk?
- Which feedback loops amplify or stabilize urban change?
- Which capacity constraints and delays matter?
- Which interventions alter the urban trajectory?
- How are outcomes distributed across neighborhoods and groups?
- What assumptions require community review?

## Companion workflow

The companion workflows compare baseline neighborhood growth, strong growth pressure, housing constraint, transport investment, congestion sensitivity, high accessibility attraction, low policy investment, and managed growth scenarios.
