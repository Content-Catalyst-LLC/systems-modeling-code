# Urban Systems Diagnostics Notes

Urban diagnostics should track trajectories, capacity pressure, access, spatial consequences, and distributional effects rather than only endpoints.

## Useful diagnostics

- Population trajectory
- Housing capacity trajectory
- Transport capacity trajectory
- Service capacity trajectory
- Accessibility proxy
- Congestion ratio
- Housing gap
- Housing pressure
- Service pressure
- Policy investment timing
- Scenario comparison
- Validation checks

The key modeling question is not whether a city grows, but whether growth interacts with housing, infrastructure, access, governance, and equity in a managed or capacity-constrained pathway.
