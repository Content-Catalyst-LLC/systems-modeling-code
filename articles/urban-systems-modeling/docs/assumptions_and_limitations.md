# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

- All data are synthetic.
- The urban growth workflow uses simplified population, housing, transport, service capacity, accessibility, and pressure relationships.
- Housing and infrastructure capacity are represented as aggregate stocks.
- Policy investment is stylized and periodic.
- Equity dimensions are represented conceptually rather than through empirical demographic data.
- Scenarios are illustrative and not calibrated.

## Limitations

- No real neighborhood, city, region, corridor, or infrastructure system is represented.
- Spatial heterogeneity is conceptual rather than geospatial.
- Real urban behavior includes politics, finance, law, culture, discrimination, informal practices, and institutional constraints.
- Applied use requires local data, spatial validation, calibration, uncertainty analysis, and community review.
